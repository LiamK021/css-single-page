INSULATION FOR NOISE REDUCTION

Unwanted sound in buildings is not only distracting, but it can also impact comfort, privacy, and productivity.

WE NEED A QUIET REVOLUTION

That's why contractors, engineers and architects choose fiberglass insulation to effectively decrease the transer of sound in residential, commercial and industrial buildings through walls, ceilings or ducts and into the spaces where people live, work and play.

Acoustic insulation comes in a variety of forms to meet your unique application requirements from batts, blankets and rolls to boards, duct liner and pipe insulation.

The same features that make fiberglass thermally efficient translate directly to the insulation's ability to absorb and reduce sound levels

NRC Value

Sound absorption capabilities are commonly expressed as a NRC value (Noise Reduction Coefficient).

The NRC value represents the percent of sound directed at the surface that is absorbed by the fiberglass insulation.

STC Value

The STC (Sound Transmission Class) rating represents the ability of an assembly to reduce sound decibel levels transferred through walls, ceiling, and floors.
